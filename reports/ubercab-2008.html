Executive Summary

Lean Invest Compelling category timing and user pain, but early operating assumptions are fragile.

Evidence pointers: p.3 value proposition, p.6 launch logistics, p.9 expansion narrative.

Key Insights

  • Strong demand signal: convenience premium appears meaningful in dense urban corridors.
  • Execution risk: marketplace balancing and driver supply reliability remain uncertain.
  • Regulatory variance: city-by-city compliance could slow scaling velocity.

Founder Questions

  1. What are driver retention assumptions after incentive normalization?
  2. Which city characteristics best predict sustainable unit economics?
  3. How quickly can legal/regulatory blockers be resolved market by market?

Action Plan

  • Request city-level cohort behavior and repeat usage trendlines.
  • Run sensitivity analysis on driver acquisition cost and idle time.
  • Prioritize markets with favorable legal runway and supply liquidity.
